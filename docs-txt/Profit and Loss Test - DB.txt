1. Priya  sold a bicycle at Rs 3600 and made a loss of 40%. At what price should she sell the bicycle if she wants to make a profit of 50%? 

Options:
A. Rs 7500
B. Rs 8990
C. Rs 9000
D. Rs 8750

Answer: C. Rs 9000.

Solution:
Let the cost price of the bicycle be x.
According to the question, Priya sold the bicycle at a loss of 40%. 
Therefore, the selling price of the bicycle is:
SP = CP - (40/100)*CP = (60/100)*CP = 0.6x
The given selling price is Rs 3600. 
Therefore, we can equate the above equation to Rs 3600 and find the cost price:
0.6x = 3600
x = 6000
So, the cost price of the bicycle is Rs 6000.
To find the selling price that will give Priya profit of 50%, we can use the formula:
SP = CP + (50/100)CP = 1.5CP
Substituting CP = 6000, we get:
SP = 1.5*6000 = Rs 9000
Therefore, David should sell the bicycle at Rs 9000 to make a profit of 50%.


2.The percentage profit earned by selling a phone for Rs. 9600 is equal to the percentage loss by selling the same phone for Rs. 6400. At what price should the phone be sold to make 30% profit?


Options:
A. Rs. 10800
B. Rs. 12000
C. Rs. 10400
D. Rs. 12500


Answer:  C. Rs. 10400.

Solution:
Let the cost price of the phone be x.
When the phone is sold for Rs. 9600, the percentage profit earned is:
[(SP - CP)/CP] * 100 = [(9600 - x)/x] * 100
When the phone is sold for Rs. 6400, the percentage loss incurred is:
[(CP - SP)/CP] * 100 = [(x - 6400)/x] * 100
According to the question, these two percentages are equal. Therefore:
[(9600 - x)/x] * 100 = [(x - 6400)/x] * 100
Simplifying the above equation, we get:
x = 8000
So, the cost price of the phone is Rs. 8000.
To make a profit of 30%, the selling price of the phone should be:
SP = CP + (30/100)CP = 1.3CP
Substituting CP = 8000, we get:
SP = 1.3*8000 = Rs. 10400
Therefore, the phone should be sold for Rs. 10400 to make a profit of 30%.

6. If selling price is doubled, then the profit triples. What is profit per cent?
Options:
A. 20%
B. 75%
C. 100%
D. 50%
Answer: C. 100%
Solution:
Let the cost price be CP and the selling price be SP.
Original Profit = SP - CP
When selling price is doubled: New Selling Price = 2SP New Profit = 2SP - CP
According to the question, when SP is doubled, profit triples: New Profit = 3 × Original Profit
Therefore: 2SP - CP = 3(SP - CP)
2SP - CP = 3SP - 3CP
2SP - 3SP = -3CP + CP
-SP = -2CP
SP = 2CP
Now, the original profit: Profit = SP - CP = 2CP - CP = CP
Profit Percentage = (Profit/CP) × 100
Profit Percentage = (CP/CP) × 100 = 100%
Therefore, the profit percentage is 100%.

16.A bakery incurs an expense of $1.50 for producing every cupcake. They also incur an additional expenditure of $500, which is independent of the number of cupcakes produced. If they are able to sell a cupcake during a promotion, they sell it for $3. If they fail to do so, they have to sell each cupcake for $1. If they produce 2,000 cupcakes, what is the number of cupcakes that they must sell during the promotion in order to break even, given that they are able to sell all the cupcakes produced?

Options:
 
A. 900
B. 800
C. 750
D. 400

Answer: C. Rs 750.

Solution:
To find the number of cupcakes that the bakery must sell during the promotion to break even, we need to calculate the total revenue and total cost and then find the point where the revenue equals the cost.
Total cost = Cost per unit * Number of units produced + Additional expenditure
Total cost = $1.50 * 2000 + $500
Total cost = $3,500
Now, let's calculate the revenue. 
Since the bakery is able to sell all the cupcakes produced, the revenue will depend on the number of cupcakes sold during the promotion and the number of cupcakes sold after the promotion.
Let's assume that the number of cupcakes sold during the promotion is x. 
Then, the number of cupcakes sold after the promotion is (2000 - x).
Total revenue = Revenue per unit * Number of units sold during the promotion + Revenue per unit * Number of units sold after the promotion
Total revenue = $3x + $1(2000 - x)
Total revenue = $3x + $2000 - $1x
Total revenue = $2x + $2000
To break even, the total revenue must be equal to the total cost. 
So we can set the two equations equal to each other and solve for x.
$2x + $2000 = $3,500
$2x = $1,500
x = 750
Therefore, the bakery must sell 750 cupcakes during the promotion to break even, given that they are able to sell all the cupcakes produced.

or, 1.15x - 0.9x - 0.225x = 2
