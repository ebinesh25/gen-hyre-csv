**1\. A modern education system should not depend on outdated methods nor rely on obsolete technology.**

Options:  
should not depend  
should neither depend  
should either depend  
No correction required

**Answer: should neither depend**

**Explanation:  
**The correlative conjunction **"neither"** must be paired with **"nor"**.  
Hence, **"should not depend"** should be corrected to **"should neither depend"** to maintain parallel structure.

**2\. She is as intelligent as or even smarter than her classmates.**

**Options:  
as intelligent  
almost as intelligent  
equally intelligent  
No correction required**

**Answer: as intelligent**

**Explanation:  
When a comparison is followed by "or even + comparative", only one "as" should be used.**

**Correct structure:  
She is as intelligent or even smarter than her classmates.**

**3\. She is the smartest of all the other students in her class.**

Options:  
student  
of all the other students  
of all students  
No improvement

**Answer: of all the other students**

**Explanation:  
**The expression **"of all the other"** is incorrect when used with a **superlative degree**.  
The correct structure is **"the smartest of all students"**, not **"of all the other students"**.

**Correct sentence:  
**She is the smartest of all students in her class.

**4\. He is interested in the history, the geography, the politics of the country.**

Options:  
history, geography and politics  
the history, geography, the politics  
the history, the geography, the politics  
No improvement

**Answer: history, geography and politics**

**Explanation:  
**When referring to subjects or general fields, the definite article **"the"** is not used.  
Hence, **"history, geography and politics"** is the correct and parallel expression.

**Correct sentence:  
**He is interested in history, geography and politics of the country.

**5\. The employee told his manager to grant him leave for two days.**

Options:  
informed his manager  
said to his manager  
requested his manager  
No improvement

**Answer: requested his manager**

**Explanation:  
**When politely seeking permission or asking for something, the appropriate verb is **"request"**, not **"told"** or **"said"**.  
Hence, **"requested his manager"** is the correct choice.

**Correct sentence:  
**The employee requested his manager to grant him leave for two days.

6.What is required is not expensive gadgets but simple tools.

are  
were  
is  
No improvement

**Answer:** is

**Explanation:  
**In sentences beginning with **"What … is/are …"**, the verb agrees with the **subject following "what"**, which here is **"required"** (singular).  
Hence, **"is"** is the correct verb.

7.The referee decided to \_**\_**\___ the match due to continuous rain.

broke into  
No improvement

**Explanation:  
**The phrasal verb **"break off"** means **to stop or discontinue an activity suddenly**.  
Hence, **"broke off"** is the correct expression to complete the sentence meaningfully.

8.I was surprised to discover that everyone was \_**\_**\____ about the sudden change in plans.

had been knowing  
had known  
knew  
was known

**Answer:** knew

**Explanation:  
**The sentence requires the **simple past tense** to report a completed state of knowledge in the past.  
Verbs like **"know"** are **stative verbs** and are not normally used in continuous forms.  
Hence, **"knew"** is the correct choice.

was held in

**Answer:** was held in

**Explanation:

11.The manager asked the employee that where he had submitted the report.

that where had he submitted  
where he had submitted  
No improvement

**Answer:** where he had submitted

**Explanation:  
**In **indirect questions**, the conjunction **"that"** is **not required**, and the sentence must follow **statement word order**, not question word order.  
Hence, **"where he had submitted"** is the correct choice.

**16\. The aim of discipline is to develop such qualities \_**\_**\__ promote self-control and responsibility.**

Options:  
as they  
who  
that  
No improvement

**Answer: No improvement**

**Explanation:  
**In expressions like **"such … as"**, **"as"** alone acts as the relative pronoun.  
The correct construction is **"such qualities as promote self-control and responsibility."  
**The option **"as they"** is grammatically incorrect because **"they"** is unnecessary.

**Correct sentence:  
**The aim of discipline is to develop such qualities as promote self-control and responsibility.

**20\. She spoke very confidently that her plan succeeded.**

**Options:  
**spoke very confident  
spoke so confidently  
spoke with very confidence  
No improvement

**Answer:** spoke so confidently

**Explanation:** The sentence requires the "so...that" construction to show cause and effect. "So confidently that her plan succeeded" correctly indicates the degree of confidence led to success. Hence, "spoke so confidently" is the correct form.

**Correct sentence:** She spoke so confidently that her plan succeeded.
